Astronomy & Exoplanets

Javed Iqbal

Astronomer & Astrophysicist

I study the detection, characterization, and habitability of exoplanets, using both photometric and spectroscopic observations to investigate planetary populations, orbital architectures, and the conditions that shape potentially habitable worlds. I also work extensively with NASA’s Kepler and TESS mission data to explore exoplanet demographics, transit timing variations, and statistical models of planetary systems,and I develop data-processing pipelines for large-scale exoplanet demographic studies.

About

I was born and raised in a small village near Sahiwal, where nights were still truly dark and the sky felt close enough to touch. I spent countless evenings watching the Milky Way drift overhead, a quiet and immense ribbon of light that sparked my fascination with astronomy long before I knew the word for it. That early sense of wonder now shapes my scientific path. I completed a BS in Physics followed by an MS in Astronomy and Astrophysics, where I began exploring the nature of distant planetary systems. My research focuses on the detection, characterization, and habitability of exoplanets. I work to understand how these worlds form, how their environments evolve over time, and what conditions might allow them to support life.

Outside of research, I enjoy hiking, writing Urdu poetry, and exploring philosophy. These interests keep me grounded and provide balance, even as my scientific work reaches toward distant worlds.

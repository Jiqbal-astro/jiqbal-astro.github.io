About

I am an astrophysicist with a focus on exoplanet detection and characterization, currently based in Pakistan. My work combines observational data, custom Python pipelines, and statistical modeling to understand planetary systems and the interstellar medium.

My research has included photometric searches for short-period Jupiter-sized planets in the open clusters NGC 884 and NGC 869, transit timing variation (TTV) analyses for additional planet detection, and Galactic H I 21 cm emission studies using small-aperture radio telescopes. I am also passionate about science communication and frequently write and speak about exoplanets and the search for life beyond Earth.

Research

My research centers on exoplanet detection and characterization, transit timing variations, and Galactic H I emission. I use optical telescopes and radio facilities, combined with custom Python pipelines and advanced statistical tools.

Exoplanets in Open Clusters

For my M.S. thesis, I performed a photometric search for short-period Jupiter-sized planets in the open clusters NGC 884 and NGC 869 using a 0.8 m telescope. While no transits were detected, the survey identified 25 variable stars (including four new candidates) and provided constraints on hot-Jupiter occurrence in young, clustered environments.


Transit Timing Variations

As a research collaborator with ÇOMÜ Ulupınar Observatory (Turkey), I lead analyses of transit timing variations in short-period planetary systems to search for additional, non-transiting planets. I develop pipelines that combine MCMC transit fitting with N-body simulations to model the gravitational signatures of unseen companions.

Galactic H I 21 cm Emission

In an independent project, I carried out 600 s drift-scan observations with the 1.5 m PICTOR radio telescope to detect and model Galactic H I emission. I implemented RFI mitigation, Gaussian profile fitting, and LSR velocity derivation using custom Python tools, obtaining column densities consistent (within ~15%) with the LAB survey.


Space Architecture & Self-Healing Materials

Within the Deep Space Initiative’s Space Architecture Program, I contribute to a Neuro-Responsive Regenerative Window System (NRWS) concept for modular space habitats. I perform thermo-mechanical modeling of adaptive self-healing materials using COMSOL and ANSYS and assess performance under orbital loading conditions.

Projects

A selection of research software, simulators, and modeling efforts that support my work in exoplanets, Galactic H I, and space-systems engineering.

Exoplanet Transit Simulator

An interactive JavaScript web application that models exoplanet transits and generates synthetic light curves including limb-darkening. Users can vary orbital parameters, explore multi-planet systems, and compare custom setups with real exoplanets such as TRAPPIST-1e, Kepler-186f, and Proxima b.

Live tool:  exoplanet-transit-simulator


Lunar & Planetary Phase Simulator

A browser-based simulator for visualizing lunar and planetary illumination phases and reflected-light curves. Features real-time Lambertian disk rendering, date-linked lunar phases, adjustable orbital parameters (inclination, eccentricity, albedo, Rp/a), and export options for PNG and CSV.

Live tool:  lunar-planetary-phase-simulator


Galactic H I Drift-Scan Pipeline

A Python pipeline built for Galactic H I 21 cm emission studies using small-aperture radio telescopes. Implements RFI mitigation, Gaussian profile fitting, and LSR velocity derivation, and cross-checks derived column densities against the LAB survey.


Transit Timing Variation Analysis Tools

Custom scripts interfacing with MCMC samplers and N-body integrators to infer the presence and properties of non-transiting perturbers via transit timing variations. Developed in collaboration with ÇOMÜ Ulupınar Observatory.

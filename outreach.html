Talks & Workshops

I give seminars and guest lectures on exoplanets, observational techniques, and radio astronomy for university students and amateur astronomy groups. I am especially interested in low-cost, open-source tools that allow students to explore professional-grade topics such as transit photometry and 21 cm spectroscopy.

Science Communication

In addition to writing and talks, I develop web-based simulators and visualizations that help students and the public understand how we detect exoplanets, how planetary phases behave, and how neutral hydrogen maps the Milky Way. These tools are designed to run entirely in the browser and require no special installation.
